How temperature conversion works

Temperature scales measure the same heat level using different starting points. Fahrenheit is commonly used in the United States, while Celsius is standard in much of the rest of the world.

To convert Fahrenheit to Celsius, subtract 32 and multiply by 5/9. To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, multiply by 9/5 and add 32.

Example

If the weather forecast says 72°F and you want it in Celsius:

(72 - 32) × 5 ÷ 9 = 22.22°C

So 72°F is about 22.22°C.

Frequently asked questions

What is the formula for Fahrenheit to Celsius?
Subtract 32 from the Fahrenheit value, then multiply by 5/9.
What is the formula for Celsius to Fahrenheit?
Multiply the Celsius value by 9/5, then add 32.
Can this converter handle negative temperatures?
Yes. You can enter negative Fahrenheit or Celsius values and convert them the same way.
